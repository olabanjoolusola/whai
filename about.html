About the Conference

About Intelligence and Imitation

Intelligence and Imitation: Mind, Mechanism, Mimesis is an inaugural interdisciplinary conference that brings scholars together to examine artificial intelligence through the lenses of philosophy, literature, history, media, culture, and human inquiry.

Conference Vision

A humanities-centered conversation about AI

This conference creates a space for rigorous reflection on AI not merely as a technical achievement, but as a historical, cultural, philosophical, and political phenomenon. It asks how traditions of imitation, mind, mechanism, authorship, and representation continue to shape the way we understand machine intelligence today.

The event is designed to foster deep exchange among scholars from multiple disciplines, encouraging conversations that move beyond narrow technical framings to include ethical, interpretive, historical, and social dimensions of AI.

Why This Event Matters

The conference focus

The event brings depth, breadth, and intellectual seriousness to current AI discussions by placing technical systems in conversation with long traditions of thought about language, creativity, intelligence, embodiment, and human culture.

Mind and Intelligence

How should we understand cognition, imitation, agency, and machine intelligence in philosophical and historical perspective?

Mechanism and Mediation

How do machines, infrastructures, models, and media systems shape what counts as knowledge, language, and creativity?

Mimesis and Culture

What can literature, art, performance, and cultural theory teach us about AI’s imitative forms and social meanings?

Host Institutions

Academic partnership and intellectual community

The conference is anchored in an academic environment committed to interdisciplinary inquiry, public humanities, and critical reflection on emerging technologies. It aims to bring together faculty, researchers, graduate students, and invited guests in a setting that encourages sustained intellectual exchange.

Conference Themes

Key lines of inquiry

History of AI

Long intellectual genealogies of computing, automation, and machine reason.

Philosophy of AI

Questions of mind, personhood, meaning, intelligence, and interpretation.

Literary Studies

Authorship, imitation, narrative, language generation, and textual culture.

Culture and Society

Power, labor, ethics, media, public life, and social consequences of AI systems.

Who This Is For

A conference for scholars across disciplines

The event welcomes participants from the humanities, social sciences, information studies, media studies, philosophy, history, literary studies, and adjacent fields interested in critical and constructive approaches to AI.

Graduate Students

Emerging researchers looking to engage a growing interdisciplinary field with fresh questions and methods.

Faculty and Scholars

Established academics seeking serious dialogue about AI beyond narrowly technical or policy-only discussion.

Interdisciplinary Practitioners

Participants interested in the cultural, historical, literary, and philosophical stakes of machine systems.
