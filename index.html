About the Workshop

Why this conference matters

This inaugural workshop examines artificial intelligence not only as a technical system, but as a cultural, historical, philosophical, and social phenomenon shaped by long traditions of imitation, mechanism, and human meaning-making.

Mind & Mechanism

Explore classical and modern ideas of mind, intelligence, cognition, embodiment, automation, and machine imitation.

Humanities of AI

Bring literature, philosophy, history, media studies, and cultural analysis into direct dialogue with AI systems and debates.

Interdisciplinary Exchange

Connect early-career researchers and established scholars around shared questions on AI’s past, present, and imagined futures.

Core Themes

Ideas driving the 2026 conversation

From antiquity to transformers, the workshop frames AI within broader traditions of imitation, representation, cognition, embodiment, and social critique.

Mimesis & Mechanism

From classical philosophy to automata and cybernetics.

Transformer Interpretation
